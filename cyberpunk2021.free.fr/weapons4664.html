FLAME THROWERS

If subtlety is not your forte, you can use this kind of weaponry, which is all the rage in short-range combat. Militarily speaking, flamethrowers are used exclusively on the battlefield, where the destruction of a building is of little importance. They are often used to dislodge the occupants of a bunker, building or armored vehicle.
Flaming liquid or napalm damage often affects several locations. They last for several rounds until the fire is extinguished. The damage given is approximate, depending on the surface of skin in contact with the fire. They recover twice as slowly as normal and require skin grafts for complete healing. Note that napalm has the particularity of causing wounds that never heal.
Only thick, rigid armor protects against this damage for a certain period of time. In any case, they lose two points per round.
